　　前回書中，講到潘三纏住蕙芳，到至急處忽有人嚷進來，蕙芳故作一驚說：「了不得了！是坊官老爺們查夜。」潘三是個有錢膽小的人，自然怕事，只得溜了。

　　原來蕙芳於下廚房時，即算定潘三今日必不甘休，即叫家裡人假裝坊官查夜，並請了兩個坊卒，到潘三歪纏不清的時候，便嚷將進來。知道潘三是色大膽小，果然中計而去，又哄過了一次。雖然得了他一個金鐲，蕙芳心中也著實躊躇，恐怕明日又來，只好到春航寓內躲避幾天，再看罷了。潘三一路喪氣而回，幸怕他的老婆，不敢公然在外胡鬧，不然只怕蕙芳雖然伶俐，也就難招架了。今天又空鬧了一場，只好慢慢兒再將銀錢巴結他，買轉他的心來。

　　這回書又要說幾個風雅人，做件風雅事情。如今這一班名士，漸漸的散了。子玉自從與琴言怡園一敘之後，總未能會面。

　　琴言之病，時好時發，也不進園子唱戲，有時力疾到怡園一走。

　　而子玉之病亦係憂悶而起，或到怡園時，偏值琴言不來；或到琴言寓裡，偏又逢著他們有事，不是他師傅請客，就是有人坐著。又不便再尋素蘭，子玉亦覺得無可奈何，只好悵恨緣慳而已。這邊琴言在家，並不知子玉來過幾次，又聽得子玉害病，心上更是悲酸，因為沒有到過梅宅，不便自去。正是一點憐才慕色之心，無可寬解，惟有短歎長吁，形諸夢寐。看官，你道子玉去尋琴言，為什麼他的師傅總不拉攏呢？一來子玉是逢場作戲，不是常在外面的人，是以長慶不相認識，且不曉得子玉是何等地位，不過當他一個年輕讀書人，無甚相與處。二來子玉在琴言身上，也沒有花過一個錢。子玉與琴言是神交心契，自然想不到這些上來。那長慶則惟在錢多，卻不在人好。那下作相公們的脾氣，總是這樣，那長慶生性如此，是始終不變的。

　　且說子玉是在家養病，不出大門，高品為河間胡太尊請去修志，劉文澤是他岳母惦記他，來接他並其室吳氏，同到直隸總督衙門去了。此中已少了三人，只有子雲、次賢、南湘、仲清、春航、王恂六人，不時往來。

　　一日，子雲、次賢招諸名士到園看龍舟，並賞榴花。此日是五月初一，正值王通政生日，雖不做壽，家中卻也有些至交好友親戚同年來賀，內裡又有些太太姑娘們，如梅宅的顏夫人，孫宅的陸夫人之類，也覺得熱鬧。王恂與仲清這怡園之約，就不能去了。是日子雲、次賢知道了，也去拜拜壽，適遇南湘、春航皆在，就約了回來。仲清、王恂說如客散得早，也來赴約。

　　但只不要候，遲早不定。次賢等應了，才回怡園，同到了迎面峭壁之下。進了一個院落，子雲便請大家寬了公服。又道：「今日天氣甚熱，紅日照人，且龍舟在吟秋水榭，榴花在小赤城，離此頗遠，不如乘馬過去。」家人們已預先備馬伺候，即帶過來，四人都乘上了。從峭壁下左手轉彎，高高低低，曲曲折折，走上青石羊腸小徑，有些古藤礙首，香草鉤衣。走完了山徑，便順著圍牆而走，那邊是池水漣漪，依紅泛綠，堤上一帶短短紅闌，修竹垂楊，還有些雜花滿樹，流鶯亂飛，已令人塵襟盡浣。不到半里，又是一堆危石，疊成高山，有十丈多高，如羅浮一峰，俯瞰海曲，擋住去路。

　　子雲請客下了馬，從山腳走上石級，三十餘層，有一小亭，中具石臺石凳，署名曰「縹緲亭」。對面望去，有幾十株蒼松，黛色參天的遮斷眼界，樹杪處微露碧瓦數鱗，朱樓一角。此間頗覺清風蕩漾，水石清寒，飄飄乎有凌虛之想。春航道：「奇奧！文心一至於此，即匡廬之香爐峰，何以過之。」南湘道：「前似王麓臺，此似蕭尺木，幽邃處卻不險仄。」子雲道：「此皆靜宜手筆，佈置時曾數易其稿。」次賢道：「也虧那幾株松樹，不然也就一望易盡。」春航道：「正不知靜宜先生胸中有多少丘壑，的是驅排河嶽神手。倪雲林、徐青藤定當把臂入林。」次賢只得謙讓幾句。四人小憩了一回，走下石磴來，側面有五間樓閣，恰作參差高下兩層，似樓非樓，似閣非閣，畫棟飛雲，珠簾捲雨，又是一番氣象。窗前闌干外，就是一個十畝方塘，內有層疊荷錢，一半成蓋。中間一座六曲紅橋，欹欹斜斜，接著對面十數間樓榭。右邊泊著幾只小小的畫船，都是錦纜牙牆，蘭橈桂槳。次賢道：「那邊就是吟秋水榭了。」再望水榭，卻是三層，左手一帶是一色楊柳低拂水面，接著對岸修竹長林，竟似兩岸欲合。

　　當下子雲讓客且慢過橋，先進那閣裡來，恰是正正三間，細銅絲穿成的簾子，水磨楠木雕闌，閣中擺設，精緻異常，說不盡寶鼎瑤琴，璇幾玉案。闌邊放一個古銅壺，插著幾枝竹箭，中懸一額，曰：「停雲敘雨之齋。」旁有一聯，其句云：拜石有時具袍笏。看雲無處不神仙。署款為華光宿。南湘失驚道：「此華公子手筆，不料其詞翰如此。」子雲道：「華公子天分極高，不過工夫稍淺，亦其勢位所誤。若論書、畫、詩、詞，倒與其境遇相反的。」春航道：「若僅聞於流俗之口，幾乎失是人矣。即此聯句，可見其胸次之雅；即此書法，可見其意氣之豪。」說罷，遠遠望見水榭邊，蕩出兩個花艇來，白舫青簾尚隔著紅橋綠柳，咿啞柔櫓之聲，宛轉彩蓮之曲，正是水光如鏡，樓臺倒影，飛燕低掠，游魚仰吹，須臾之間已過紅橋，慢慢攏橋，慢慢攏過來。只見王蘭保掖起羅衫，盤了辮髮，鬢邊倒插一枝榴花，手中拿一根小小的紫竹篙，一面撐，一面趕那些家鳧野鴨，倒驚得鴛鴦、溪鳥亂飛起來。又有一個白鷺鷥，竟迎著闌干翩然而來，到了簷前，把翅一側，已飛上山岩去了。

　　次賢笑道：「所謂『打鴨驚鴛鴦』，今日見了。」大家正看得有趣，又見船中走出幾枝花來。一隻船內是寶珠、漱芳，一隻船內是蕙芳、素蘭，共是五個。舟人把舟泊近闌干，南湘道：「芙蓉未開，水榭減色。有此眾芳一渡，庶不寂寞。湘娥洛神，江湄遊戲，我度香先生當以玉佩要之。」大家笑了一笑，群旦上來都見過了。次賢道：「你們看靜芳窄袖踟躕的，越顯得風流跌宕。竹君之贊語『翩若驚鴻，婉若游龍』，真覺得摹擬入神。」南湘道：「靜芳之倜儻，媚香之靈慧，瑤卿之柔婉，瘦香之妍靜，香畹之丰韻，皆是天仙化人。若以其藝而觀，則趙飛燕之掌上舞，張靜婉之帳中歌，可以彷彿。」子雲請客登舟，南湘等上得船來。看那船頭，是刻著兩個交頸鴛鴦，船身是棠梨木的，兩邊短短紅闌，內是玻璃長窗，篷蓋上罩著個綠泥灑花大卷篷，兩邊垂下白綾畫花走水。船裡是兩個艙，底下鋪了細白絨毯，靠後也是長窗，中間鋪設一炕，兩旁是鬼子穿藤小椅，間著幾張茶几，中間一張圓桌，也可以坐得五六人。那一個船略小了些，是坐那侍從人的。此時王蘭保卻早換好了衣裳，斯斯文文的坐了。寶珠對南湘道：「你們早上到過王大人家沒有？」南湘尚未回言，子雲道：「我就在王宅邀來的。」

　　於是眾人談談講講，一路看園中的景致，有幾處是飛閣凌霄，雕甍瞰地。有幾處是危崖突兀，老樹槎木牙。卻也望見西北上一帶長廊是桃塢，接著是杏村；正北上竹林中望去是梨院，後是牡丹香國；東北是一帶玲瓏巧山，下是綠陰千樹，金彈離離，結滿了梅子，青黃各半，把個梅崦遮住，看不清楚。對岸樹石蒙茸，卻不知還有多少亭院。春航問南湘道：「這園子裡共遊過幾處了？」南湘道：「到卻到過許多回，逛卻沒有逛到。一喝酒就是一天，那裡能逛。約有七八處逛過。」寶珠道：「我同瘦香是逛完的了。」蕙芳道：「我就是桂嶺、菊畦、蘭徑沒有到過，其餘也都逛完。」

　　素蘭道：「桂嶺在前山前，蘭徑、菊畦是在後山後，過澗去一片大空地，有一所莊院，便是菊畦。那蘭徑是山下，到半山，高高下下的長廓曲徑，最好頑的所在。菊畦過去還有個稻莊。有桔槔戽水，像個村落，漁簾蟹籪，各樣都有。還有兩個鶴欄、鹿棚，也近在那裡。」

　　說罷船已行了半里多，已到轉彎處，池水卻也空闊。吟秋水榭造在水中，四面周圍有池水圍住，共是三層；只見第一層是十二間，作個六面樣式，面面開窗，純用玻璃鑲嵌的雕窗，隔作六處。一處之中又分陰陽明暗，仍是十二處，大小方圓扁側，又不一樣，各成形勢。內中的擺設，是說不盡的。在這間，看那間只隔一層玻璃，到過去時，卻要轉了好幾處，方能過去。當下諸人，就在這第一層逛了好一回時候。子雲道：「客也餓了，此刻將近午正，可以坐罷。」只見四個小童托上四個金漆盤來，放著幾碗杏酪，分送各人面前，各人吃了。春航道：「索性上那兩層再回來坐罷。」於是轉上樓梯，上了第二層，略小了些，是四面樣式，空出一轉回廓，有闌干迴護，也有雕窗隔作八處，古玩器皿一樣的精雅。望見東北角上柳陰中，泊著龍舟，有三丈多高，舟身子是刻成彩畫一條青龍，中間卻是五六層架子裝起，純用五彩綢緞綾錦氈泥，製成傘蓋旗幡，繡的灑線平金打子各種花卉，還搭配些孔雀泥金傘、珍珠傘、銀針穿成的傘，中間又裝上些剪彩樓臺庭院，王宮梵宇，裝點古蹟。內中人物都是走線行動，機巧異常。一層一層的裝湊起來，為錦為雲，如荼如火。頂上站著一個紮成的金毛孔雀，船內用石壓底，兩邊共有二十四人蕩槳。有個八音班，在內打動鑼鼓絲竹，粗細十番。此是次賢在江蘇看過，畫出圖樣，選匠造製。春航是從南邊來，也曾見過，即道：「實在製得華麗，就是常州府的龍舟，是甲於一省的，也不過如此。」

　　大家又上了第三層，卻是三面式樣，外面也是三面回廓，中間隔作六處。此中窗櫥門戶，是一色香楠木，十分古拙，更為雅靜。地位既高，得氣愈爽，憑闌一望，怡園的全景已收得八九分，只有山陰處尚不能見。惟覺樓臺層疊，花木扶疏，芳草如碧毯平鋪，清泉如水銀直瀉，水如縈帶，山列主賓，多處不見其繁，少處不嫌其略。天然圖畫，輞川圖不過如斯。人力經營，平泉莊何足道也。眾人各自憑闌，遙望四處，只聽龍舟內簫鼓悠揚，清波蕩漾的划將出來。

　　龍尾上掛著個鞦韆架子，兩個孩子一上一下的打鞦韆。次賢道：「還請到底下去看罷，自上望下，不如自下望上好。」眾人即下了雁齒扶梯，仍到第一層，已見正中廓前擺了一個圓桌。此會是賓主四人，名花五人。子雲便要穿衣，經史、田三位止住，只得就便服送了酒，依齒而坐。東首是南湘，子雲命蘭保坐在肩下。西首是春航，肩下是蕙芳。上面是次賢，肩下是漱芳。

　　子雲坐了主位，左右為素蘭、寶珠二人。飲酒的話頭，無非是那幾套，且慢講他。

　　再看那龍舟已到閣前，盤盤旋旋，來來往往，蕩個不了。

　　家人遠遠的放了五千一串的全紅百子，響得不住。大家正看得喝采，忽見闌干外走上四個人，穿著綠油綢短衫，紅油綢褲，赤膊拴腰，紅巾紮額，赤了腳，穿著草鞋，腿上纏緊了藍布，站齊在闌干前，對上叩了一個頭。南湘不解其故，待要問時，只聽龍舟一聲鼓響，那四個人齊齊的倒翻筋斗下水去了。子雲道：「這些蠢奴，他們也要顯些本領。」遂命家人去捉幾對鴨子來，又叫取幾個紅漆葫蘆拋下水去，眾人方曉得是奪標。家人答應，便將一個白鴨先拋下水去，那鴨子下了水，把頭一鑽也翻了一個筋斗，便伸著頭，拍著翅，呷、呷、呷的叫了幾聲。

　　那邊一人便俯在水面，兩腳一蹬，似梭子的穿過來。那鴨子見人來拿他，便扇起雙翅，半沉半浮，走得風快。正走時，忽見水裡探出個頭來，一手把鴨子捉住。子雲道：「好！記著賞他。」又將三隻鴨子，兩個葫蘆同拋下去。這四個人各要討好，都竭盡其藝，或俯或仰，或沉或浮，或側半面，或蹺一腿，游來游去，頑個不了。也有拿著的，也有拿不著的，也有拿到了，重新脫手的，也有拿到半路，被人奪去的，引得席上個個歡笑，各人飲了好幾杯。那些相公們更覺高興，都出了席，靠著闌干看玩藝。

　　子雲叫了進來，再斟了酒。次賢道：「我們今日就以此為令何如？」眾人問道：「怎樣做令？」次賢問那些家人道：「去年園中結那些大葫蘆，想來還有。」家人應道：「有十幾個漆的，其餘是沒有漆的。」次賢便叫把漆的拿來。不多一刻，家人就提了一大串來，解開繩子，放在一張空桌上。次賢又叫拿那副酒籌來。家人又送上一個象牙酒籌。次賢隨手抽出幾枝，便把沒有字的一面朝上，放在桌上，對眾人道：「各人隨手取一根，不准看那一面的字，各人注上各人的號。」大家就依了他。

Across the river were more pavilions and courtyards hidden among trees and rocks. Chunhang asked Nanxiang, “How many places have you visited in this garden?” Nanxiang replied, “I’ve been here many times, but I’ve only toured about seven or eight places. Whenever we drink, it takes the whole day, leaving no time for exploring.” Baozhu said, “Shouxiang and I have seen it all.” Huifang added, “I haven’t been to Osmanthus Ridge, Chrysanthemum Path, or Orchid Lane, but I’ve seen the rest.”

Sulan said, “Osmanthus Ridge is in front of the mountain, Orchid Lane and Chrysanthemum Path are behind the mountain. Across the stream is a large open space with a village, which is Chrysanthemum Path. Orchid Lane is halfway up the mountain, with winding corridors and paths, the best place for playing. Beyond Chrysanthemum Path is a rice farm with water wheels and fishing nets, resembling a village, complete with crane enclosures and deer pens nearby.”

As they chatted, the boat sailed for over half a mile, reaching a wide open area of the pond. The Yinqiu Pavilion stood in the water, surrounded by the pond. It had three stories; the first had twelve rooms in a hexagonal layout, each with glass windows and intricate designs, divided into twelve sections, each uniquely shaped. The furnishings inside were too numerous to describe. They toured the first floor for a while. Ziyun said, “Guests must be hungry; it’s almost noon. Let’s sit down.” Four boys brought golden lacquer trays with bowls of almond pudding for everyone. Chunhang suggested, “Let’s visit the other two floors before we sit down.” They climbed to the second floor, which had a four-sided design with a balcony, and the interior was divided into eight sections with elegant decorations. In the northeast corner, they saw a dragon boat decorated with colorful paintings, flags, and ornaments. Chunhang, who had seen similar boats in the south, commented on its splendid design, rivaling the best in Changzhou.

They climbed to the third floor, which had a three-sided design with balconies. The windows and doors were made of fragrant nanmu wood, giving it a rustic and elegant feel. The higher elevation offered a refreshing breeze and a panoramic view of Yiyuan, except for the shaded areas. The scenery resembled a natural painting, with overlapping pavilions, lush flowers and trees, clear springs, and winding streams. The beauty of the garden was comparable to famous paintings and man-made landscapes.

As they admired the view, they heard the melodious sound of drums and flutes from the dragon boat below. The boat, adorned with a swing at the tail, had children playing on it. Cixian suggested they go down to see it up close. They descended the ladder to the first floor, where a round table was set in the central hall. The four hosts and five performers sat around, with Ziyun at the head. They served wine and sat according to rank, with Nanxiang and Ziyun at the east, Chunhang and Huifang at the west, and Cixian and Sufang at the top. Ziyun sat in the host’s seat, flanked by Sulan and Baozhu.

As they drank and chatted, the dragon boat slowly approached the pavilion, moving gracefully on the water. Servants set off strings of firecrackers, adding to the lively atmosphere. Suddenly, four men in green oilcloth jackets, red trousers, and red headbands, barefoot and wearing straw sandals, stood before the railing and bowed. Nanxiang, puzzled, was about to ask when the dragon boat sounded a drumbeat, and the four men somersaulted into the water. Ziyun said, “These silly servants want to show off their skills.” He then ordered servants to fetch some ducks and red lacquer gourds for a game. The servants threw a white duck into the water, which dived and resurfaced, flapping its wings and quacking loudly.

One person leaned over the water and kicked his feet, shooting through the water like a shuttle. Seeing someone coming to catch it, the duck flapped its wings, half-submerging and floating, moving swiftly. As it swam, a head suddenly emerged from the water, and a hand grabbed the duck. Ziyun said, “Good! Remember to reward him.” Then he threw three more ducks and two gourds into the water. The four men, eager to impress, used all their skills—diving, floating, flipping sideways, or kicking one leg—swimming back and forth, putting on quite a show. Some caught the ducks, some didn’t, some caught them only to lose them again, and some had their catch snatched away mid-swim, causing everyone at the table to laugh and drink several cups of wine. The actors were even more delighted, leaving their seats to lean against the railing and watch the spectacle.

Ziyun called everyone back in to pour more wine. Cixian suggested, “How about we make this the game for today?” The others asked, “What’s the game?” Cixian asked the servants, “Those large gourds that grew in the garden last year, are there any left?” The servant replied, “We have about ten lacquered ones; the rest are unvarnished.” Cixian ordered the lacquered ones to be brought. Soon, a servant came with a large string of gourds, untied the string, and placed them on an empty table. Cixian also called for the wine tokens. The servant brought an ivory set of wine tokens. Cixian randomly drew a few and placed them face down on the table, saying to the group, “Everyone pick one without looking at the other side. Mark your own token.” They all complied.

Cixian then opened the gourds, placing one token inside each, and sealed them tightly, instructing the servant to throw them into the water. “Whoever retrieves one will have to drink. It’s a fair game.” Everyone agreed, curious about the inscriptions on the tokens. Cixian explained, “These tokens feature characters from the ‘Water Margin,’ each with a drinking story. I don’t know which names are on them.” Ziyun laughed, “This sounds fun. But some of these characters are known for their heavy drinking, so it might be challenging.” The others agreed, “We’ll just have to see what happens.”

One gourd was retrieved, and the servant wiped it dry and opened it. The token belonged to Xiao Cixian. Everyone saw the inscription, which read, “Li Kui causes havoc at Xunyang River.” The note said, “The first two seats belong to Song Jiang and Dai Zong, the last seat to Zhang Shun. Li Kui drinks a big cup, Song and Dai drink small cups, and Zhang Shun gets ten punches. If Li Kui wins, Zhang Shun drinks; if Zhang Shun wins, Li Kui drinks water.” Everyone laughed. Cixian drank his cup first, with Nanxiang and Chunhang accompanying with their cups. Then he and Ziyun played a drinking game, resulting in Ziyun drinking six cups of wine and Cixian drinking four cups of tea. The group found it amusing.

Another gourd was brought up, and the token belonged to Nanxiang. The inscription read, “Wu Song gets drunk and takes over Happy Forest.” The note said, “Drink three cups first. Then, facing Jiang Menshen, win three consecutive rounds to pass.” Nanxiang said, “That’s too much. I’ll drink the three cups but skip the rounds.” Ziyun objected, “No skipping, especially since you’re a hearty drinker.” Lanbao suggested, “For the rounds, maybe half a cup each time?” Everyone agreed. Nanxiang drank three cups and then won three rounds against Chunhang, ending up drinking twelve cups in total.

Two more gourds were retrieved, and the first token was Ziyun’s. The inscription read, “Song Jiang kills Yan Poxi.” The note said, “Drink two cups. The companion, Yan Poxi, is to drink one. If she refuses, Song Jiang drinks both.” Ziyun joked, “Who is Yan Poxi here?” Everyone laughed. Cixian remarked, “It’s your neighbors; you must drink and persuade them.” Ziyun laughed, drank one cup, and handed the other to Sulan, saying, “Xiangwan, don’t be like Yan Poxi, only caring about Zhang Sanlang. Drink this cup.” This made everyone laugh. Sulan, initially willing to drink, blushed and nodded towards Baozhu, saying, “Yan Poxi is over there; ask her to drink.” Baozhu laughed and took the cup, drinking it while teasing Sulan.

The next token belonged to Huifang. The inscription caused everyone to laugh, except Tian Chunhang, who forced a smile with a slightly red face. The token read, “Pan Jinlian plays with her uncle in the snow.” The note said, “Three cups. The left-hand companion is Wu Song. The first cup must be offered with exposed chest and a hand on Wu Song’s shoulder, saying, ‘Uncle, drink this cup.’ The second cup must be half-drunk, then say, ‘Uncle, if you care, drink the rest.’ The third cup must be drunk standing up, acting angry, with everyone accompanying with three cups.” Huifang refused, “Let’s skip these three cups.” Ziyun insisted, “The rules are clear. It was your idea.” Cixian added, “You wrote these rules yourself; no backing out now.” Nanxiang remarked, “If we change the rules now, what’s the point?”

Huifang, reluctant, hesitated for a long time. Lanbao laughed, “You see, karma is quick. Now it’s your turn to play Pan.” The group, unaware of the story about Pan San, thought it was just the token’s Pan Jinlian. Chunhang, understanding, remained silent. Huifang, blushing even more, had no choice but to take a cup and lean towards Baozhu, saying, “Uncle, drink this cup.” Baozhu, caught off guard while eating, laughed, spraying food everywhere, and leaned on Ziyun, wiping his mouth with a towel. Everyone roared with laughter. Huifang, embarrassed, put the cup down and laughed as well. Cixian remarked, “Meixiang, you got it wrong. The note says the left-hand companion is Wu Song, not the right-hand one. Why offer the cup to Yaoqing?” Huifang, flustered, asked, “Who should I offer it to then? He’s sitting next to me.” Baozhu said, “I’m not exactly beside you. Even though it’s a round table, I’m facing north, and you’re facing east. I never expected you to call me uncle.” Everyone laughed again, but Huifang still refused. Ziyun smiled, “Meixiang, haven’t you ever offered Xiangfan a drink? Do it quickly! Look, they’ve brought up two more tokens. If you break the rule now, what about later? Just do it this once, it won’t come around again.” Cixian urged, “Quick, offer the drink!” Nanxiang added, “When Pan Jinlian played with her uncle, she had to show some charm, not be so serious.”

Everyone chimed in, pressuring Huifang. She had no choice but to lean towards Chunhang, raising her cup, and said, “Uncle, drink this cup.” Chunhang, reluctantly, drank it. For the second cup, Huifang drank half and offered the rest to Chunhang, who drank it without waiting for her to call. The group said, “That doesn’t count, you need to call him.” Chunhang pleaded, but they insisted. For the third cup, Huifang poured and stood up. Cixian said, “This one needs to show anger.” Sulan remarked, “She already has some anger.” Huifang laughed, composed herself, and acted angry, downing the cup in one go. Ziyun, impressed, drank three cups himself and encouraged the group to drink three cups as well.

Next, they looked at the token, and it was Lanbao’s. On it was written: “Lu Zhishen drunkenly beats the monastery gate.” The note said: “Drink a large cup first, then the first two seats, being the Arhats, each compete in three rounds of finger-guessing.” Huifang complained, “He gets it easy while mine was so troublesome.” Lanbao followed the instructions, competing in three rounds each with Nanxiang and Chunhang.

The next token was Sufang’s. On it was written: “Jin Cuilian sings in the tavern.” The note said: “Play the pipa and offer a cup each to Lu Da, Li Zhong, and Shi Jin.” Everyone commented, “That’s reasonable, not too demanding. And how coincidental that it involves someone named Jin.” Sufang played the pipa and offered cups to Nanxiang, Chunhang, and Cixian.

Then, they checked the gourd containing Tian Chunhang’s token. Chunhang quickly glanced at it and exclaimed, “Oh no!” The others laughed and said, “Today, it seems Meixiang and Xiangfan are entangled.” Huifang, blushing, said, “This is deliberately done; why are these two tokens the only ones like this?” Cixian responded, “Unfair accusation! If I had picked them on purpose, how could you have gotten them?” The token read: “Yizhangqing captures Wang Shuanghu.” The note said: “Later becoming husband and wife, hold a red scarf together with the neighbor, drink three cups cross-handed, and everyone else toasts one cup.” Chunhang wanted to change the rule but couldn’t resist the group’s insistence. He reluctantly handed a handkerchief to Huifang, and they each drank half a cup, causing much laughter and jokes. The third time, the whole table toasted, causing Huifang great embarrassment. She remarked, “My unlucky streak is finally over. Now, I can tease others.” The next token belonged to Baozhu, and it read: “Madam Wang speaks flirtatiously upstairs.” The note specified three cups: the first offered to the right-hand neighbor as Ximen Qing, mimicking a curtain-lifting gesture with a fan; the second to be offered to Ximen Qing while kneeling, holding Jinlian’s shoe tip; the third to the left-hand neighbor as Madam Wang, with a bow and greeting “Dry Mother, drink this cup.”

Ziyun laughed, “Now it’s my turn.” Chunhang joked, “It’s delightful to have incense dust on the knees. Ten cups would be fine.” Nanxiang laughed, “As long as you don’t kneel in the mud and grab a donkey’s leg instead of the lotus hook.” This caused an uproar of laughter, with Jin Sufang laughing so hard she fell into Cixian’s arms, and Wang Lanbao and Lu Sulan walked away laughing. Baozhu remarked, “This is retribution; the heavens are just.” Chunhang felt awkward but could only endure it. He jokingly said, “Zhujun, you deserve a penalty for being so mean.” Nanxiang laughed, “I was just stating the truth, not being mean.” Huifang said, “Enough talking, you know everyone has their moments.” Cixian laughed, “Yaoqing, this rule can’t be changed, so you’ll have to endure it. Poor Duxiang.” This caused another round of laughter.

Huifang urged Baozhu to quickly offer the drink. Baozhu, a gentle person, reluctantly used the fan to lightly tap Ziyun, offering the drink. Ziyun accepted it with a smile, and everyone cheered and drank. Ziyun said, “Wine rules are stricter than military rules, so no complaints. Gentlemen, don’t laugh at me.” He then poured a cup of wine, knelt on one knee, and offered it to Baozhu, who hurriedly accepted and drank. Everyone cheered again and drank. Baozhu offered the third cup to Huifang, bowing and saying, “Dry Mother, please drink this cup.” Huifang accepted it with a smile, saying, “Good daughter, thank you.” Everyone praised, “What a good mother and daughter pair! Let’s toast again.” They all drank another cup.

Finally, the last token was Sulan’s. It read: “The heroes gather at Liangshan Marsh.” The note said: “Everyone drinks three cups.” The group commented, “That’s a fitting end, and today’s drinking game seems perfectly arranged.” Baozhu remarked, “It’s too easy for him, and for Jingfang, while Shouxiang had to play the pipa. Only Meixiang and I had it tough.”

Huifang responded, “Someone knelt to offer you a drink, isn’t that good enough?” Baozhu retorted, “If you wanted someone to kneel, why didn’t you take my place?” By this time, the sun was setting, and they ate dinner.

After washing up, they drank fragrant tea. Nanxiang suggested, “We haven’t admired the pomegranate flowers at Little Chicheng. Why not go now, with the evening glow illuminating them?” Ziyun led the group back on the boat, crossing the red bridge to disembark at the western rockery. They walked through the Immortal Cave, passing through Apricot Tower and Peach Grove to reach Little Chicheng. There, they saw a hundred or more pomegranate trees blooming like a fiery city, with red hues flickering and seemingly ablaze, interspersed with yellow, white, and agate-colored flowers. It was like the scene described in “The Rhapsody of Mount Tiantai,” thus named Little Chicheng.

As night fell, Nanxiang and Chunhang prepared to leave. Servants brought their coats and hats, and they thanked the hosts and Cixian before leaving the garden. Ziyun mentioned, “I had something to ask you two. The garden needs more couplets in various spots, but we were delayed by the dragon boat today. Let’s arrange another visit soon and invite Yuxiang and Jiantan as well.” Both Shi and Tian agreed and left by carriage. The five performers also gradually dispersed. This second banquet at Yiyuan, though not fully attended, leaves us wondering who will be featured in the next chapter. Let’s wait and see.
